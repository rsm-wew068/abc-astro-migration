## Why Bathrooms Punish Cabinets

The bathroom is the most demanding humidity environment in a home. A hot shower can push relative humidity to the ceiling in minutes, then the room dries out for hours. That constant swing is what destroys cheap cabinetry. Particleboard boxes swell at the edges, veneers lift, and unsealed MDF wicks up water at the base until the corners crumble. None of that shows on day one, which is why bathroom cabinets are where cutting material corners costs you the most.

The fix is not exotic. It is the right substrate and a finish that seals every surface. We build vanity boxes from furniture-grade plywood, which holds its shape through humidity cycles far better than particleboard. Painted doors use solid wood frames around an MDF panel, and every edge, including the sink cut-out, gets sealed so water has nowhere to enter. That combination is what separates a vanity that looks new in ten years from one that swells in two.

## Planning the Vanity Around the Plumbing

The single biggest layout challenge in a vanity is the plumbing. The drain and supply lines eat into the cabinet interior, which is why so many stock vanities waste the space under the sink. Custom construction lets you plan around it. Drawers can be built with a U-shaped notch that wraps the P-trap, turning dead space into usable storage for toiletries. A false drawer front at the top can hide a tilt-out tray for sponges by the basin.

Think in zones. A bank of full-extension drawers on one side stores everything small, from cosmetics to hair tools, with power routed to an interior outlet so a dryer or electric toothbrush charges out of sight. A door cabinet on the other side takes tall bottles and cleaning supplies. Getting this split right is worth more day to day than any finish decision, because it determines whether the vanity is genuinely useful or just a box with a sink on top.

## Floating, Freestanding, or Furniture Style

Bathroom vanities come in three broad forms, and the room usually points to one. A wall-mounted floating vanity leaves the floor visible underneath, which makes a compact San Francisco bathroom read as larger and simplifies mopping. It needs solid in-wall blocking to carry the weight, so it is easiest to specify during a remodel while the wall is open. A floating vanity pairs naturally with a modern, frameless look and slab or flat-panel fronts.

A freestanding, furniture-style vanity sits on the floor on legs or a toe kick and can feel more traditional, especially with a framed, inset door and a stone top. For a primary suite where you want a piece that anchors the room, this is often the better choice. The third form is the built-in run, where the vanity extends into linen storage or a makeup counter as one continuous unit. That continuity is where custom cabinetry pays off, because stock vanities come in fixed widths that rarely fill a wall cleanly.

## Finishes and Hardware That Survive Daily Use

Finish choice in a bathroom is about durability first and style second, though you do not have to sacrifice either. A quality painted finish or conversion varnish resists moisture and cleans up easily, which matters near a sink that sees toothpaste and hand soap every day. Thermofoil and laminate fronts are also strong performers in a bathroom because their surfaces are effectively waterproof, and modern versions look far better than the plastic-looking laminates of the past. For a natural look, a well-sealed wood veneer in white oak or walnut brings warmth without the upkeep of an open-grain finish.

Hardware deserves the same attention it gets in a kitchen. Soft-close hinges keep doors from being slammed against a damp frame, which protects the finish over time. Full-extension, soft-close undermount slides let drawers pull all the way out so nothing gets lost at the back, and they handle the weight of a loaded drawer without sagging. Pick handles and knobs in a finish rated for wet environments, since cheaper plating can pit and corrode in a steamy room. These are small parts you touch every single day, so quality here is felt immediately.
