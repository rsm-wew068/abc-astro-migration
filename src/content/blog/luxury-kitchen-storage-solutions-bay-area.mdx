## Rethink the Base Cabinets: Drawers Over Doors

The fastest upgrade in usable storage is converting lower cabinets from doors-and-shelves to drawer banks. A base door cabinet looks like it holds a lot, but the back third is hard to reach and everything ends up stacked, so you pull out three pots to get the one at the bottom. A deep drawer on full-extension slides brings the whole contents to you at once. You see it, you reach it, and nothing hides in the back corner.

The build details are what make this feel like luxury rather than just function. We build drawer boxes from dovetailed solid maple or Baltic birch, which hold heavy stacks of cast iron and stoneware without loosening or racking. They ride on full-extension, soft-close undermount slides rated for the load, so a drawer full of dishes glides out fully and closes itself with a quiet pull. That combination, dovetail box plus undermount soft-close slide, is the baseline we design around for every base cabinet.

## Solve the Corners and the Dead Zones

Every kitchen has spots that default to wasted space, and a custom approach is largely about reclaiming them. Blind corners, where two cabinet runs meet at ninety degrees, swallow storage into an unreachable void. A blind-corner pull-out or a swing-out system brings that back third out into the room where you can actually use it. It is not cheap hardware, but it turns dead cubic feet into daily-use space.

The other overlooked zones are vertical. Taking upper cabinets all the way to the ceiling adds a full shelf of seasonal or rarely-used storage and eliminates the dust-collecting gap on top. Toe-kick drawers under base cabinets recover a few inches of flat storage for baking sheets and platters. A narrow pull-out filler beside the range or cooktop holds oils, spices, and utensils within arm's reach of the stove. In tight San Francisco and Peninsula kitchens, these small recoveries add up to the difference between cramped and comfortable.

## The Pantry, Done Right

A well-built pantry is one of the clearest markers of a considered kitchen. The luxury version is not necessarily a giant walk-in, which many Bay Area floor plans cannot spare the square footage for. It is often a tall cabinet fitted with full-height pull-out units that bring shelves toward you from both sides, so a single unit five to seven feet tall gives you complete visibility of everything from front to back with one motion. Nothing gets lost behind a can of tomatoes from two years ago.

For narrower runs, a full-height slide-out larder in a nine to twelve inch cabinet tucks serious storage into a space too tight for a swinging door to be useful. Adjustable shelving matters here: we build on drilled shelf-pin systems so the interior can be reconfigured as your needs change, and we size shelf depth to real pantry goods rather than a generic stock spacing. Because everything is built to your cabinet's exact interior, there is no gap where a stock organizer almost fits.

## Prep, Cleanup, and the Details You Touch Daily

Luxury storage is really about the choreography of cooking. A dedicated cutlery and utensil drawer with fitted dividers near the prep zone means you are not rummaging. A deep drawer with an adjustable peg system holds plates and bowls securely and adjusts as your dishware changes. Next to the oven, a vertical tray divider stores sheet pans, cutting boards, and cooling racks upright instead of in a leaning pile.

Cleanup deserves the same attention. A pull-out bin system with separate compartments for landfill, recycling, and compost, mounted on heavy-duty slides near the sink, keeps bins off the floor and out of sight, which is both cleaner and safer with kids or pets around. We build the surrounding cabinet to the bin system rather than shoehorning bins into a stock box, so the fit is exact and the slides carry the weight properly. These are the moving parts you interact with thousands of times a year, and getting them right is what a custom kitchen from a local shop is actually for.
