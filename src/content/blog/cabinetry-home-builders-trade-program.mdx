## Why Local Manufacturing Changes Your Schedule Math

Most national cabinet lines quote 6 to 10 weeks, and that clock often doesn't start until the order clears their queue. Because we manufacture in San Jose rather than ship from out of state, our standard lead time runs about three weeks from approved shop drawings. For a builder running multiple units, that difference compounds fast. You can stage cabinet delivery to match drywall and floor completion instead of ordering months ahead and eating storage costs or job-site damage.

Proximity also matters when something goes sideways. A door arrives with a finish flaw, or a field measurement changes because a wall moved during framing. We can remake a component and get it back to your site in the Bay Area within days, not weeks. Trying to resolve a warranty or replacement issue through a distant factory and a distributor middleman is where schedules quietly die.

## What Comes Standard, and Where You Can Spec Up

Our trade cabinets are built to hold up in real households, so the baseline is not the stripped-down RTA quality you find at big-box stores. Standard construction includes 3/4-inch plywood boxes rather than particleboard, dovetail solid-wood drawer boxes, full-extension undermount soft-close slides, and soft-close hinges. Doors and face frames use solid hardwood, with plywood or MDF center panels depending on the door style and finish.

From there, the program scales to the project. For spec homes and rental turns where budget discipline matters, semi-custom lines in standard sizes keep per-unit cost predictable. For higher-end developments and custom homes, we build fully custom sizes, specialty interiors, and finish-matched panels for appliances and islands. Finishes range from painted MDF doors that resist telegraphing to stained and clear-coated hardwoods. If your architect specified a particular door profile or a color to match an existing palette, we match it in-house rather than sending you to a limited catalog.

## How the Trade Account Actually Runs

Setting up a trade account starts with a conversation about your volume and typical project type, whether that's a 30-unit townhome development in San Jose, a series of San Francisco flat remodels, or custom homes on the Peninsula. Approved trade partners get tiered volume pricing, dedicated project management, and consistent point-person contact so you're not re-explaining your standards on every job.

The workflow is straightforward. You send plans or field dimensions, we produce shop drawings and a quote, and nothing goes to the shop floor until you sign off on drawings and finish selections. That approval step protects both of us: it catches the wall that moved, the appliance that changed, the client who wants a different pull. For multi-unit work, we can lock a standard cabinet package across units so every kitchen is identical, then flag the handful of upgraded units separately. Delivery is scheduled to your site in San Jose, San Francisco, or San Mateo to align with your build sequence.

## Quality Control That Keeps Callbacks Off Your Punch List

Every callback after close-out costs you a trade day and a bit of your reputation with the buyer. We inspect boxes, doors, and finishes before anything leaves the shop, checking for consistent color across a run, square boxes, and hardware that operates cleanly. Because we control the build locally, we catch problems at the source instead of discovering them when a homeowner opens a drawer six months in.

Our cabinets carry warranty support, and because you're dealing with the manufacturer directly, a warranty claim doesn't bounce between a dealer and a factory. That matters most on developments where you're managing a warranty period across many units. If a soft-close mechanism fails or a finish issue appears, we handle the replacement part and coordinate directly, which keeps the resolution fast and keeps your client relationship intact.
